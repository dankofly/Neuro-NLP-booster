# Meta-Programs, VAK Predicate Banks, Logical Levels, Values & Beliefs

How to detect a reader's mental sort and write copy that slides past their critical filter. Match the sort and
the message feels native; mismatch it and the reader feels subtly unheard even when the content is identical.

---

## 1. Meta-Programs (perception filters) — copy cues per pole

For broad audiences, serve multiple poles in sequence (global promise up top → detail/proof mid → options vs
steps in the offer). For a known 1:1 prospect, mirror their dominant pole.

### Toward / Away-From (motivation direction) — the master axis
- **Detect:** Toward = goals, gain, "so I can…", growth, plenty. Away = "I don't want…", "stop…", risk, loss, fear.
- **Toward copy:** state the wanted end-state positively. "Become slim and fit" not "stop the weight battle." Verbs: achieve, gain, unlock, get.
- **Away copy:** lead with pain escaped, risk avoided, cost of inaction. "What happens if nothing changes?" Verbs: avoid, prevent, stop losing, no more.
- Most sales pages hit BOTH: agitate the away pain, then sell the toward dream.

### Options / Procedures
- **Options:** wants choices, freedom, possibilities. "Choose the plan that fits you." "Endless ways to…" No lock-in.
- **Procedures:** wants the proven step-by-step path. "Our proven 5-step system." Numbered roadmaps, "the right way to X."

### Internal / External (frame of reference)
- **Internal:** decides for self, resists being told. "You'll know it's right when you see it." "Only you can judge." Hand evidence, don't dictate the conclusion.
- **External:** needs others' proof. Testimonials, ratings, "trusted by 10,000", expert endorsement, "as featured in".

### General / Specific (chunk size)
- **General:** big concepts, vision, the overview. Headline with the big promise, don't drown them in detail up top.
- **Specific:** details, numbers, names, sequences. Concrete sensory evidence, specs, comparison tables, fine print.

### Match / Mismatch (sameness vs difference)
- **Sameness:** "works just like X, only easier", continuity, familiar metaphors, gradual upgrade.
- **Mismatch:** "unlike anything you've used", category-creation, novelty. Note: mismatchers instinctively find the counterexample to any claim, so phrase as questions or invite their own conclusion.

### Proactive / Reactive
- **Proactive:** imperative CTAs, momentum. "Start today." "Take the first step now." Single decisive button.
- **Reactive:** permission + understanding first. "Here's how it works, then decide." FAQs, evidence before the ask, low-commitment first step.

### Self / Other
- **Self:** "what you'll gain", personal transformation, your status, your freedom.
- **Other:** "what this does for the people you lead/love/serve."

### Time orientation (Past / Present / Future) + Timeline
- **Past:** heritage, "trusted since", "go back to what worked."
- **Present:** urgency, "available now", "today only", scarcity windows.
- **Future:** vision, "imagine 12 months from now", aspirational future-self.
- **Move the future closer** to manufacture urgency: "This time next month, you'll already be…" Readers who feel the future "within their grasp" act; those who see it "miles away" don't.

### Other polarities
- **Plenty / Scarcity:** abundance buyer → "more, growth"; scarcity buyer → "protect what you have, don't lose, secure."
- **Right-focus / Wrong-focus:** right → "build on strengths"; wrong → "fix what's broken."

## 2. VAK Predicate Banks (match the reader's sensory channel)
Web rule: Hero = visual-heavy (decision is a glance). Body proof = auditory (testimonials, quotes). CTA + guarantee
= kinesthetic ("get started", "secure your spot", "feel confident"). Rotate all three for broad audiences.

**VISUAL:** imagine, focus, look, see, picture, view, notice, show, reveal, clear, bright, vivid, illustrate,
perspective, in light of, appears, outlook, observe, vision, foresee, transparent, crystal clear, blind spot,
mind's eye, snapshot, spotlight, get the picture, see what I mean, brilliant.

**AUDITORY:** hear, listen, tell, say, talk, sounds, ask, ring a bell, tune in, on the same wavelength, loud and
clear, word for word, click, harmony, in tune, resonate, rings true, voice, call, articulate, vocal, unheard of,
music to my ears, clear as a bell, hear me out, so to speak.

**KINESTHETIC:** feel, grasp, touch, hold, handle, solid, grip, catch on, get a handle on, hard, smooth, concrete,
pressure, sensitive, warm, cold, tap into, get in touch, sharp, soft, firm, comfortable, heavy, weight off your
shoulders, gut feeling, sink in, stand firm, hands-on, support, momentum, anchored.

**OLFACTORY-GUSTATORY** (primal, memory-loaded, use sparingly): fresh, sweet, sour, bitter, taste, savour, flavour,
mouth-watering, sniff out, leave a bad taste, recipe for, juicy, spicy, food for thought, sweet smell of success,
sink your teeth into, hunger for, craving. Strongest anchors for nostalgia and desire.

## 3. Dilts Logical Levels (pitch copy at the right altitude)
Low→high, each level shapes the one below and is more powerful to change:
Environment → Behavior → Capability → Beliefs/Values → Identity → Purpose/Mission.

- Most copy lives at Behavior/Capability ("does X, has feature Y"). Premium, category-defining, brand-loyal copy
  operates at **Identity and Purpose**. Pitch one level above competitors.
- **Identity-level (highest leverage):** don't sell the product, confirm who the buyer is.
  - Behavior: "Track your runs." → Identity: "You're a runner." → Purpose: "How your work connects to something bigger."
- **Behavior precedes belief:** "Easier to behave your way into new thinking than think your way into new behaving."
  Get the reader to take ONE tiny action (free trial, one habit); identity follows. Onboarding > persuasion essays.

## 4. Values Elicitation & Value-Laden Language
Values drive motivation more than features. Run these on your ICP (interviews, reviews) to build the value lexicon,
then write copy in their value-words verbatim.
- **Hero question (deepest values surface here):** "Whom do you most admire, and what's important to you about
  that person?" The traits a buyer admires in a hero are the traits they want to embody. Mirror those back.
- **Peak-experience question:** "Describe a time you were at your absolute best." Capture every word, narrow to top 3.
  Those are core motivational drivers.
- **Rule:** sell to the value above the feature. If the value is freedom, every feature ladders to "more time back."

## 5. Beliefs & Belief Change (objections are limiting beliefs)
Beliefs pre-filter perception ("believing is seeing"); buyers delete evidence that contradicts a belief. You can't
argue a belief away with features; reframe, then trigger a tiny confirming action.
- **Common limiting beliefs = objection archetypes:** "it won't work for me", "I have no power to change this",
  "I can't afford it", "now's not the time", "I can do it myself", "people like me don't get this."
- **Always respect the old belief first** (it protected something). Attacking it = attacking identity = defensiveness.
  "Your caution has protected you. Here's how to keep it and still move."
- **The lever is a tiny action, not a better argument.** Free trial / one small win → belief updates itself.
- **Find the load-bearing false assumption and swap it** ("success depends on track record" → "success depends on present performance").
- **Box-9 conversion secret (secondary gain):** most non-action comes from what the buyer GAINS by staying stuck,
  or fear of loss-through-success. Name it and resolve it: "You might worry scaling means losing the simplicity you love. It doesn't, here's how."
- **Seligman ABCDE for nurture sequences:** Adversity → Belief (pessimistic story) → Consequences → Disputation → Energization.

## 6. Convincer Strategy (how a person becomes convinced)
**Channel (what must be satisfied):** See (demo, screenshots, before/after, video) / Hear (testimonials, "what people
say") / Read (case studies, specs) / Do (free trial, interactive demo, money-back guarantee = "try the feeling").
**Frequency (how many proofs before convinced):** multi-proof skeptics need 3-5 proof points; auto-believers need one
strong proof; time-types need a trial/drip; consistent-types need ongoing proof + low-risk re-entry.
A high-converting page runs them in sequence: visual proof → testimonials → case detail → trial/guarantee, with a fast
lane for one-proof deciders and depth for the skeptics.

---

## 7. State Management (the reader's state determines how they read)
Match → Pace → Lead. Match where they ARE (frustrated, skeptical, tired); don't fake-cheer a grumpy reader. Pace
briefly so they feel understood. Then lift YOUR energy; they follow to stay in rapport. Pacing length scales with
intensity (longer match for skeptical/low-energy; fast match then de-escalation for angry).
- Rewrite the reader's inner voice: replace "I'll mess this up" with the exact empowering line ("I can do this, and here's the tool").
- Step-into-the-future-self: stage the aspirational self and invite the reader to step in.
- Dissociation to defuse risk: "view yourself from across the room / two years out" drops fear before the ask.
