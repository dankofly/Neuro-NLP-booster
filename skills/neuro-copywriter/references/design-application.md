# Design Application: Matching Visual Design to the Motive

Copy and design must fire the SAME motive or they cancel (incongruent signals get suppressed; congruent signals
across channels feed a ~10x neural multiplier). For any visual brief, deliver a **Design Spec** with these eight
dimensions, all tuned to the one chosen motive. Hand it to a designer or to an imagegen/frontend skill.

## The Design Spec template (fill per asset)
1. **Target motive + Limbic Type** (one, committed).
2. **Color palette** — primary, accent (the anchor color, used on every CTA), background, with the motive rationale.
3. **Typography character** — serif/sans, weight, contrast, what feeling it must carry.
4. **Form language** — round/organic vs angular/sharp vs minimal/geometric.
5. **Imagery direction** — subject, mood, what the hero image must depict (and what to avoid).
6. **Layout & density** — spacing, hierarchy, where the eye lands first (the <2s motive trigger).
7. **Motion & interaction** — animation energy, "self-doing" elements for retention.
8. **Sound** — sound logo / micro-interaction sounds (the most underused lever).

## Per-motive design systems

### BALANCE / Harmonizer / Traditionalist
- **Color:** warm muted, brown / soft green / warm beige / gold / muted blue (trust). Dark tones calm. Low saturation.
- **Type:** humanist serif or soft rounded sans, medium weight, generous line-height. Warm, approachable, not clinical.
- **Form:** round, soft, closed, organic, symmetric. No sharp edges or aggressive diagonals.
- **Imagery:** family, home, generations, nature, craft, familiar everyday warmth, community. Real people, soft light.
- **Layout:** calm, spacious, clear structure, no overwhelm. Trust signals dense and visible (seals, guarantees, testimonials).
- **Motion:** gentle, slow fades. **Sound:** warm, soft.
- **Trigger:** the visitor must feel safe and "among their own" in <2s.

### DOMINANCE / Performer
- **Color:** black + deep red + metallics (silver/anthracite), strong contrast, gold for status. Bright = activating.
- **Type:** bold, high-contrast, condensed or assertive sans, tight tracking. Uppercase for power lines.
- **Form:** angular, sharp, vertical/ascending, heavy, massive (heavy = valuable).
- **Imagery:** summits, status symbols, victory, technology, power poses, before/after ascent, beating rivals.
- **Layout:** clear dominance hierarchy, big confident type, comparison tables (winning), premium pricing shown as a status signal.
- **Motion:** fast, decisive, sharp. **Sound:** deep, powerful (low motor rumble = strength).
- **Trigger:** the visitor must feel this is the elite, winning choice.

### STIMULANCE / Hedonist
- **Color:** bright, saturated, high-contrast, unusual combos, glowing accents, gradients.
- **Type:** expressive, playful, variable, unexpected pairings, dynamic sizing.
- **Form:** dynamic, asymmetric, playful, in-motion, unexpected.
- **Imagery:** novelty, movement, variety, lifestyle, creativity, pattern interrupts, color pops.
- **Layout:** surprising, broken grids, discovery-driven, gamified elements, limited-edition badges.
- **Motion:** lively, animated, interactive (high "self-doing"). **Sound:** upbeat, surprising micro-sounds.
- **Trigger:** the visitor must feel "this is new and exciting, I want to explore."

### ADVENTURE/THRILL (Dom+Stim) / Adventurer
- Strong red/orange/black, dynamic sharp forms; imagery of extremes, nature-as-adversary, solo hero, speed, storms.

### FANTASY/ENJOYMENT (Stim+Bal) / Open-Minded
- Soft warm tones, pastel, flowing forms; imagery of sensuality, enjoyment rituals, soft textures, relaxation, aesthetics.

### DISCIPLINE/CONTROL (Bal+Dom) / Disciplined
- **Color:** blue (cool, factual), grey, anthracite, reduced palette, lots of whitespace.
- **Type:** clean precise sans, structured, even weight, technical clarity.
- **Form:** straight, geometric, minimal, grid-strict.
- **Imagery:** function, technology, order, clarity, data. No fluff or emotion-bait.
- **Layout:** structured, evidence-forward, specs and comparison, fact-based microcopy.
- **Trigger:** the visitor must feel this is the rational, no-nonsense, well-engineered choice.

## Cross-cutting design neuro-rules
- **<2-second motive trigger:** hero image + dominant color + form + headline must all hit the SAME motive instantly.
- **Anchor color:** one accent color on every CTA, kept constant, becomes the buying-state anchor.
- **Emotional hero image > product shot.** Show the after-state / a relevant key visual that fires the reward center, not a neutral product render.
- **Heavy = valuable:** use depth, shadow, weight for premium feel; light/flat reads cheap.
- **Self-doing for retention:** configurators, hover reveals, interactive demos, quizzes (80-90% retention vs 20-30% passive).
- **Disarm the Balance brake** visually: trust seals, guarantee badges, social-proof counters near the CTA.
- **Code stability:** keep the core color/logo/key-visual constant across page, ad, email; vary only the periphery for novelty.
- **No vampire effect:** every striking visual must pay into the brand/motive, never just grab attention for its own sake.
- **Layout as spatial anchor:** consistent geography (price after value-stack, CTA same position, testimonials beside claims).

## Handoff
When the user wants the design actually built, pair this spec with a frontend/design skill (e.g. `frontend-design`,
`high-end-visual-design`, `imagegen-frontend-web`) and pass the motive, palette, type, form, and imagery direction
as the brief. For image generation, translate the Imagery direction into a concrete prompt (subject + mood + light +
color + composition) tuned to the motive.
