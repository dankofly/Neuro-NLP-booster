# NeuroScore Engine + the Hook Engine

Mirrors and extends the Hooka scoring system. Every asset gets scored 0-100 on four metrics; you then optimize the
lowest. This is the quality gate that separates engineered copy from "nice writing."

## The 4 NeuroScores (0-100 each)

### 1. Pattern Interrupt
Does the opening break the reader's autopilot? The brain pays immediate attention only when an expected pattern is
violated. Score high when the first line is unexpected, contrarian, or breaks a category convention. Score low when
it opens with a generic claim, a greeting, or "we are a leading…".

### 2. Emotional Intensity
Depth of limbic activation. Score high when strong, specific emotion is evoked (shock, relief, pride, fear, desire,
belonging) tied to the target motive. Score low for flat, feature-first, "professional" neutrality. Negative stimuli
score higher on raw activation (loss aversion), but must resolve into the toward-dream.

### 3. Curiosity Gap (Zeigarnik)
Open loops the brain instinctively demands to close. The Zeigarnik effect: an unfinished story/question stays active
in memory and pulls the reader forward. Score high when the copy opens a loop it doesn't immediately resolve ("The
one thing nobody tells you about X…"), uses cliffhanger structure, withholds the mechanism. Score low when everything
is stated flat and closed.

### 4. Scarcity / FOMO
Urgency pressure. Score high for genuine scarcity (limited spots/time/quantity), social proof of others acting,
cost-of-waiting, and present-moment triggers. Score low when there's no reason to act now. Must be true scarcity;
fake countdowns get learned away and poison trust (the discount-fatigue effect).

> Report as: `Pattern Interrupt 82 / Emotional Intensity 74 / Curiosity Gap 68 / Scarcity 55`. Name the weakest, say
> why, rewrite to lift it, re-score. The Hooka brief also lets you set TARGET scores per asset (e.g. a trust-heavy
> Balance page might target Scarcity 40, Emotional Intensity 80); match the target profile to the motive, don't max
> everything blindly. Maxing FOMO on a Balance/Traditionalist audience backfires (triggers their risk brake).

## The Hook Engine (5 Pattern-Interrupt types, from Hooka)
Pick the mechanism that stops the scroll for THIS audience:
1. **Contradiction** — state the opposite of the reader's belief. "Posting more content is killing your reach."
2. **Paradox Reversal** — a logical impossibility that intrigues. "The faster you try to grow, the slower you go."
3. **Provocative Denial** — "Don't read this if you're happy with your conversion rate."
4. **Unexpected Truth** — a true thing nobody says out loud. "Most landing pages fail in the first 2 seconds, before a word is read."
5. **Rule Breaking** — violate a category norm openly. "We're going to tell you not to buy the bigger plan."

Hook construction levers: curiosity gap + specificity (a number, a timeframe) + a trigger word. Trigger-word bank
(weave verbatim): *finally, most people don't realize, unnoticed, hidden, immediately, hardly anyone, right now,
the truth about, before you, what nobody tells you.*

## Other retention mechanics to layer
- **Open loops / nested loops:** open a loop early ("in a second I'll show you the exact line that doubled this"), resolve later. Stack 2-3.
- **Specificity bias:** exact numbers out-convert round ones (37% beats "a lot", "0.8 seconds" beats "fast").
- **Cliffhanger transitions** between sections so the reader keeps scrolling.
- **The "but/so/therefore" spine:** never "and then, and then." Each beat earns the next via tension (but) or consequence (so).

## Scoring workflow
1. Draft per the neuro-arc.
2. Score all four metrics, honestly (don't flatter the draft).
3. Identify the single lowest score = the bottleneck.
4. Apply the matching fix (weak Pattern Interrupt → swap in a Hook-Engine type; weak Zeigarnik → open a loop; weak
   Emotional Intensity → max submodalities + Limbic word-set; weak Scarcity → add a true constraint or cost-of-waiting).
5. Re-score. Ship when all four clear the target profile for the motive.
