# Neuromarketing Principles: Brain, Codes, Story, Senses, Price, Web Rules

Source: Häusel, *Neuromarketing*. The science layer beneath the copy and design.

## 0. Core laws
1. **No emotion, no money.** Stronger positive emotion = higher willingness to pay. Money is stored emotion; the brain offsets the emotion-value of money against the emotion-value of the offer.
2. **70-95% of every decision is unconscious (implicit).** The conscious "I" is a press secretary justifying decisions it didn't make.
3. **Emotion before cognition.** Limbic system fires <200ms, before cortical image processing. Un-emotional input often never reaches consciousness.
4. **Pain avoidance > pleasure gain.** Negative stimuli trigger higher activity. Loss aversion is neural.
5. **Ads work without conscious attention or explicit memory.** Attention is a LATE filter. Surveys measure the wrong channel; conversion is the verdict.
6. **Multisensory enhancement:** consistent message across channels = up to 10x amplification (superadditive). Incongruent signals get suppressed.

## 1. Autopilot vs Pilot (System 1/2)
| | Autopilot (System 1) | Pilot (System 2) |
|---|---|---|
| Capacity | ~11M bits/s | ~40 bits/s |
| Code | "Action" | "Think" |
| Content | emotions, heuristics, brand associations, pattern recognition, intuition | working memory, cost-benefit, language, planning |
| Speed | instant, unconscious | slow, effortful |

The Autopilot takes over when buyers are time-pressed, info-overloaded, low-involvement, or uncertain (two similar
brands). That's the normal state: ~95% of ad contacts are low-involvement. **Contact times are brutal: ad 1.7s,
banner 1s, email relevance check 2s. A landing page must activate the right motive implicitly in <2 seconds.**
Never argue explicitly against deeply learned implicit codes (McDonald's "health" failure); build on them.

## 2. Brand Codes (Scheier/Held) — the bridge to motives
Brands live as neural networks. Two levels: **Motive level** (Balance/Stimulance/Dominance = relevance &
differentiation) and **Code level** (the perceptible signals). **Indirect rule:** never address motives directly
(a Porsche driver doesn't want his status motive named); dock onto them via codes that transport the motive implicitly.

**4 code carriers of meaning:**
1. **Sensory** — color, form, sound, light, typography, haptics.
2. **Episodic** — told stories, shown episodes ("Freude am Fahren").
3. **Symbolic** — protagonists, figures, gestures, locations, logos (Beck's three-master = adventure).
4. **Linguistic** — written/spoken word (headline, copy, voice-over).

**Implementation rules (avoid costly errors):**
- 48% of ads get attributed to the wrong/no brand without a logo → wasted spend feeds competitor networks. Branding test: hide the logo, is it still recognizable?
- Consistency premium: consistent patterns across senses = neurons fire up to 10x.
- Code stability: the brain re-learns brand signals slowly (min ~2 years). Keep core signals; vary only the periphery. Frequent overhauls = expensive (Camel).
- Learn via dosed irritation: the brain learns when the known integrates with the new. But every deviation alarms the dACC (conflict monitor) → creative concepts fall through in market research ("creativity killer"); distrust survey opinions.

## 3. Reward system, dopamine, expectation
The ventral striatum (nucleus accumbens) fires on reward EXPECTATION, before reward arrives. Marketing sells the
expectation. Strong brands light up the inner reward system (orbitofrontal cortex) like the sight of one's own
children. **Discount symbols** fire the striatum AND dampen cognitive control (anterior cingulate) → buy probability
rises; it works UNCONSCIOUSLY (people deny falling for it, then buy). But overused discounts get learned away.
Web: visualize the reward expectation early (dream outcome), use discount symbolism sparingly, confirm "you saved X."

## 4. Storytelling (brain-friendly)
The brain stores patterns and recalls them as stories. "We are memory." Storytelling is one of the most powerful tools.
- **Beginning + end matter most.** The start decides attention, the end sets the emotional marker.
- The brain tolerates no sense-gaps. Keep "connectability" high.
- **Stories need heroes** ("without heroes our self wobbles"); hero need not be human. The customer is the hero, the product is the helper/the sword against the dragon.
- **"Without disturbance, no story."** Structure: core message + plot + disturbance + characters.
- Plot polarities that grip: life/death, arrival/farewell, love/hate, good/evil, security/fear, truth/lie, strength/weakness, hope/despair.
- "Not true, but fitting." Networks run on fits/doesn't-fit. Web: above-the-fold = story start (hook, disturbance/problem); CTA = the end (emotional marker, reward).

## 5. Multisensory & sensory design (Lindstrom)
- Multisensory enhancement / superadditivity: consistent message on several channels = up to 10x (not addition).
- Retention rates: hearing ~10-20%, seeing ~20-30%, see+hear ~40-50%, **see+hear+do ~80-90%** ("of what we do ourselves we keep 90%").
- Universal implicit meanings: heavy = valuable, light = cheap; dull door-thud = exclusivity; bright = activating, dark = calming; crisp apple-snap = freshness.
- Form signals carry gender/value codes before content (the waist/hourglass mayonnaise form beat the Buddha form 100%).
- **Web application:** optimize SOUND first (underused, easy: sound logo, micro-interaction sounds, hover feedback). Simulate HAPTICS (texture visuals, material renders, "weight" via shadow/depth). Maximize "self-doing" (configurators, hover, drag, quiz) for highest retention + binding. Keep codes consistent across page/ad/email/sound. Define the core vision first, then optimize sense by sense (never change everything at once).

## 6. Price, cognitive biases, heuristics
- **Loss aversion:** price increases register more than equal decreases. "Don't lose X" often beats "gain X."
- **Reference dependence / anchoring:** prices judged relative to a reference. Use struck-through RRP, embed in a costlier contrast.
- **Cognitive-load surcharge:** hard-to-process prices feel higher. Make prices easy to process.
- **Price figure/rounding effects**, contrast/anchoring.
- **High vs low involvement:** expensive products engage the hippocampus more (deeper processing, less spontaneous). Cheap = fast, easy decision.
- **Insula (pain) vs limbic "want":** purchase is a conflict. Strong enough "want" (strong brand/emotion) accepts higher prices.
- **First-choice-brand effect:** per category per person, often only ONE brand emotionalizes the decision process. Aim to be number one; second place already lacks that power.
- **Source amnesia:** content and source are stored separately; ads work even when the spot is forgotten.

## 7. Web / landing-page rules (AIDA is dead)
Perception and evaluation run SIMULTANEOUSLY, not sequentially. Attention is a late filter. The "impact maxim"
(sex/celebs/shock) backfires when unrelated to the brand (**vampire effect**: the attention element eats the brand).
Every signal must pay into the brand core.
1. **2-second rule:** above-the-fold activates the target motive implicitly via hero image + color + form + headline, all consistent.
2. **Motive consistency, no "dead middle":** every element pays into ONE motive. Define the target motive profile, then check each code.
3. **Emotional hero images > product shots.** Relevant key visuals that fire the reward center.
4. **Visualize reward expectation early** (dream outcome), not only at the end.
5. **Use the multiplier:** consistent codes across page, ad, email, sound.
6. **Peripheral effect:** non-focused elements (background, color fields, sound) are learned implicitly. Design the atmosphere on purpose.
7. **Disarm the Balance brake:** trust signals, guarantees, social proof, especially on Dominance/Stimulance offers.
8. **Loss-framing + easy-to-process prices**, anchoring via struck-through RRP/contrast.
9. **Code stability:** keep brand codes (core color, logo, sound, key visual) constant; vary only the periphery for novelty.
10. **Story build:** hook/disturbance up top (heaviest weight), emotional marker at the CTA. User = hero.
11. **Build in "self-doing"** (interaction, configurator, quiz, hover) for 80-90% retention.
12. **Never write against deeply learned codes**, play around implicit prejudices with your own codes.

## 8. Gender, age, culture (for targeted copy/design)
- **Gender:** men → testosterone/vasopressin → Dominance + Adventure + Discipline; respond sharper to status/competition/erotic stimuli. Women → estrogen/oxytocin → Balance + Care + Bonding; social motives. Women decide ~70% of household spend but ~80% of campaigns are made by men (a gap = an opportunity). Overlapping curves, no black/white.
- **Age:** testosterone + dopamine fall → curiosity, risk, status drive decline (Stimulance + Dominance weaken); cortisol rises → Balance dominates. Golden-age marketing wins by better need-fit, not louder volume.
- **Culture:** same systems, different strengths/expression. West = individual/either-or, focuses the main object; Asia = group/both-and/harmony, holistic (background matters), Balance dominant + Dominance second (status via luxury). Colors flip (red = luck in China, blue = "evil", white = mourning). Check name translations and color meanings for international work.

## 9. Ryte: SEO-meets-conversion (copy that ranks AND converts)
- **WDF*IDF (semantic relevance, not stuffing):** rarer terms describe a document better. Reverse-engineer the relevant term set from top-ranking pages and cover the full semantic field naturally. Works for content/informational/long-tail; fails for transactional heads and mindless insertion.
- **Holistic content:** one deep page answering the whole question cluster beats ten thin pages. Strong promise/answer up top (intent + skimmers), then progressively deeper sections covering every sub-question, objection, and use case.
- **Search intent alignment:** informational → educate first, soft CTA; commercial → comparison + proof + objection-handling; transactional → minimal friction, price clarity, dominant CTA. Mismatched intent kills ranking AND conversion.
- **Heading hierarchy = the conversion path.** Each H is a question the buyer asks; answer it and lead to the next. Schema markup (FAQ/Product/Review) → rich results → higher CTR before conversion even starts. Speed + accessibility are conversion levers, not just SEO.
- **Synthesis:** skimmable AND deep, global promise + proof up top for fast intent-satisfaction (dwell time), progressive depth below for the considered buyer. Meaning over frequency; never stuff.
